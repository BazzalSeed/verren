# Tyler's three flows — advisor source material

Source: Tyler Calder (Partner, Cerity Partners; Verren advisor), relayed by Seed
on 2026-07-19. This is the advisor's-eye view of where a practice actually needs
help, in his words and tool references — the primary source behind the
flow-first positioning explored at `/claude` (see `src/content/claude.ts`).

His framing note on prospecting: **"that's where the money is — but we can do
both."**

## The flows (verbatim structure)

### Prospecting

- Analyzing current portfolios in yCharts
- Creating custom proposals using models
- Creating custom pitch books using intranet
- Ensuring follow-up and pipeline management using CRM

### Onboarding

- Creating 12–18 month client roadmap based on discussion notes (Zocks and Outlook)
- Account opening based on investment strategies
- Tracking document checklist (trusts, tax returns, etc.)

### Ongoing

- Task management and follow-up (CRM)
- Meeting scheduling (Outlook)
- Financial planning (eMoney)
- Trading (Red Black)
- Money movement (Schwab/Fido)

## How this maps to Verren's positioning (interpretation, not Tyler's words)

- The three flows are the **revenue lifecycle** of a practice: win the client →
  start the relationship → keep the relationship. This is the outcome framing
  behind the `/claude` variant's pitch.
- Trading and money movement are the consequential actions — in every use of
  this material they stay **approval-gated** per the copy guardrails
  (`src/i18n/copy.ts` header): shown as waiting on the advisor, never autonomous.
- Tool names are integration framing ("inside the tools your firm already
  uses"), not partnership claims — we have no relationship with these vendors
  and must not imply one.
