# AUM-linked TAM addendum — sourced numbers

*Compiled 2026-07-14. Every figure carries its source URL and data year. Intended to splice into the one-pager TAM section.*

## 1. The asset base: advised US retail AUM

- **Total SEC-registered RIA AUM (all clients, incl. institutional): $144.6T (2024) → $176.8T (2025, +22.3%).** 16,544 SEC-registered advisers serving 73.7M clients; 61.0M individuals now use an adviser for asset management (more than doubled over eight years). — IAA/COMPLY Investment Adviser Industry Snapshot 2026 ([GlobeNewswire, Jun 2026](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/03/3306149/0/en/2026-Investment-Adviser-Industry-Snapshot-Shows-Continued-Growth-in-Demand-for-Adviser-Services.html); [IAA industry statistics](https://www.investmentadviser.org/industry-snapshots/); prior-year figures: [InvestmentNews, 2025](https://www.investmentnews.com/ria-news/industry-snapshot-more-advisors-more-clients-1446t-aum/260706)). **Do not use this as the retail TAM anchor** — it includes funds, institutions, and double-counted sub-advised assets.
- **Retail advisor-managed assets (the honest anchor): $31.3T as of 2023**, across all channels (wirehouse, IBD, hybrid/independent RIA, insurance, bank B/D), grown at a 7.3% 10-yr CAGR. — Cerulli, *U.S. Advisor Metrics* ([report page, 2025 ed.](https://www.cerulli.com/reports/us-advisor-metrics-2025); [info packet PDF](https://s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/cerulli-website-assets/documents/Info-Packs/2025/Cerulli-US-Advisor-Metrics-2025-Information-Packet.pdf)). At that CAGR, ~$36–38T by 2025–2026.
- Cross-checks: fee-based **managed account assets alone = $13.7T (YE 2024**, +19.8% y/y; flat through Q1 2025) — Cerulli/MMI ([Cerulli press release, 2025](https://www.cerulli.com/press-releases/managed-account-assets-reach-13.7-trillion); [MMI Q1 2025 data](https://www.mminst.org/insight/mmi-cerulli-q1-2025-advisory-solutions-data)). Total US household financial wealth **>$90T (YE 2024)**, >$102T est. YE 2025 — Cerulli ([press release, 2025](https://www.cerulli.com/press-releases/u.s.-household-total-financial-wealth-exceeds-90-trillion)).

## 2. The fee on those assets → advisory revenue pool

- **Average advisory fee ≈ 0.96% of AUM** — 2026 *State of Financial Planning Fees* study, Datos Insights + Envestnet MoneyGuide (cited in [NerdWallet, 2026](https://www.nerdwallet.com/financial-advisors/learn/how-much-does-a-financial-advisor-cost)).
- **Median fee at a $1M portfolio ≈ 1.02%; 100–120 bps typical under $1M, 80–100 bps at $2M+** — Kitces Research on advisor fee structures ([kitces.com](https://www.kitces.com/blog/financial-advisors-charge-services-fee-structure-advisory-firm-profession-aum-pricing-insight/), 2024–2025 data).
- **Implied US retail advisory revenue pool: ~$31T × ~0.8–1.0% blended ≈ $250–310B/yr.** (Blend below headline 1% because large accounts pay less.) This is the pool advisor software takes its cut from.

## 3. Precedent: wealth platforms already price in basis points, not seats

- **Envestnet** — ~**$6.2T platform assets, 19.4M accounts** (Q2 2024, [SEC 8-K earnings supplement](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1337619/000133761924000018/q224earningssupplement.htm)); FY2023 revenue **$1.245B, with asset-based recurring revenue = 60% of total** ([Envestnet 10-K FY2023](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1337619/000133761924000005/env-20231231.htm)). Taken private by Bain Capital (2024) at ~$4.5B.
- **AssetMark** — **$108.9B platform assets, record $545M net revenue, FY2023** → an implied **~50 bps net revenue yield** on platform assets ([Q4-2023 release](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2024/02/21/2833145/0/en/AssetMark-Reports-108-9B-Platform-Assets-for-Fourth-Quarter-2023.html); [10-K FY2023](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1591587/000162828024011239/amk-20231231.htm)). Taken private by GTCR (2024) for ~$2.7B.
- **Addepar** — AUM-based SaaS pricing of roughly **0.8–3 bps of assets on platform** (higher for firms <$350M AUM; e.g., ~$65K/yr for a $220M-AUM firm) — [Sacra company profile, 2024](https://sacra.com/c/addepar/).
- **Orion** — **$5.2T AUA** as of mid-2025, +35% since 2023 ([Business Wire, Jul 2025](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250722136152/en/)); pricing is a flat fee that scales with AUM ([platform pricing comparison, 2026](https://x1wealth.com/compare/advisor-platforms-2026)).
- Takeaway for the one-pager: the monetization unit in wealth software is **the basis point on advised assets**, not the seat. Even 1 bp on the $31T retail advised base is a **$3.1B/yr** revenue lane; the platform incumbents above collectively prove willingness to pay from Addepar's ~1–3 bps (reporting only) up to AssetMark's ~50 bps (full TAMP stack).

## 4. Analyst sizing of the overall wealth-tech spend

- **Global wealth management IT spending: ~$56.1B (2024), forecast $66.9B by 2028 (4.5% CAGR)** — Celent, *Wealth Management IT Spending Forecasts by Domain, 2023–2028* ([celent.com](https://www.celent.com/en/insights/270528835); [related Celent forecast note](https://www.celent.com/en/insights/946479393)). North America is the largest region.
- Narrower "wealthtech solutions" software-market sizings run **$5–8B (2024–2025) growing ~14–15% CAGR** ([Market Research Future, 2025](https://www.marketresearchfuture.com/reports/wealthtech-solutions-market-24442); [Mordor Intelligence, 2025](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/wealth-tech-solution-market)) — useful as a conservative floor; both dwarf the advisor-CRM-only slice.

### Caveats
- The $176.8T IAA figure and the $31.3T Cerulli figure answer different questions; always pair them explicitly ("industry AUM vs. retail advised AUM") or the TAM reads inflated.
- Cerulli's $31.3T is 2023; label projections beyond it as extrapolations.
- Envestnet/AssetMark/Orion post-2024 financials are private-equity-owned and no longer disclosed; latest public figures are cited above.
