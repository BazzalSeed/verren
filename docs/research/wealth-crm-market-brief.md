### Bottom-up TAM math

Observed ACV anchors (see §2 pricing): Wealthbox ~$470–540/user/yr, Practifi ~$960–1,340/user/yr, Salesforce FSC ~$2,700–5,400/user/yr.

- **US CRM-replacement TAM (advisor seats only):** ~300k advisors × $600–$1,500 blended ACV = **$180M–$450M/yr**.
- **US CRM TAM (all RIA seats):** ~1.0M non-clerical employees × $500–$1,000 = **$0.5B–$1.0B/yr** (staff/ops/compliance seats are real CRM seats; Redtail's per-database pricing exists precisely because firms have many non-advisor users).
- **AI-expanded ACV case:** AI notetaker + workflow tools already layer ~$1,200+/advisor/yr on top of CRM (Jump/Zocks-class pricing); an AI-native CRM that absorbs the notetaker, meeting prep, and follow-up automation plausibly commands $2,000–$3,000/advisor/yr → 300k US advisors × $2,500 = **~$750M US**, ~2× that globally.
- Sanity check vs. budgets: RIA tech spend runs **3.8–6% of revenue** ([InvestmentNews benchmark via CircleBlack](https://www.circleblack.com/key-ria-industry-statistics/); [Kitces range via COMPLY](https://www.comply.com/resource/how-to-select-technology-for-a-new-ria-firm-step-2-setting-a-budget/)), with solo advisors budgeting **$6k–$10k/yr total** on tech ([Investipal](https://www.investipal.co/blog/7-technology-investments-financial-advisors-should-make-before-2026)) — so a $1.5k–3k AI-CRM line item fits inside existing budgets, especially since 54% of advisors planned to *increase* tech budgets ~19% in 2025 ([Orion WealthTech Survey via Revisor](https://revisorgroup.com/advisor-technology-in-2025-building-smarter-infrastructure-for-independent-firms/)).

### Top-down analyst sizing

- Global **wealth management CRM** market: **$12.5B (2024) → $30.2B (2033), 10.5% CAGR** ([Verified Market Reports](https://www.verifiedmarketreports.com/product/wealth-management-crm-market/)); IMARC pegs it reaching **$14.0B by 2033, 10.9% CAGR** ([IMARC](https://www.imarcgroup.com/wealth-management-platform-market)). These use broad definitions (banks, insurers, private banks), so treat as ceiling.
- Global **wealth management software**: **$5.5B (2024) → $12.07B (2030), 14% CAGR** ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/wealth-management-software-market)).
- Reconciliation: the bottom-up US independent-advisor CRM wedge ($0.5–1B) is a slice of the multi-billion top-down category; the delta is enterprise wirehouses, banks, and non-US private wealth. Growth in every top-down report is attributed to AI/ML integration ([IMARC](https://www.imarcgroup.com/wealth-management-platform-market)).

## 3. Problem thesis: the pain is documented

- **Advisors are mostly not advising.** Kitces Research: advisors spend only **~41% of time on client-facing activities**; the rest goes to admin, data entry, compliance documentation, and "hunting for information scattered across disconnected systems" ([Kitces via Digital Alpha](https://www.digital-alpha.com/integration-before-ai-what-the-2025-kitces-research-really-says/); [Kitces time study](https://www.kitces.com/blog/how-do-financial-advisors-spend-time-research-study-productivity-capacity-efficiency/)).
- **Meeting prep is the single biggest sink.** Typical advisor spends **~36% of time** on meeting prep, planning analyses, and post-meeting servicing ([Kitces](https://www.kitces.com/blog/how-do-financial-advisors-spend-time-research-study-productivity-capacity-efficiency/)); "for every one hour of client meetings, at least two hours of preparation" ([Human Interest](https://humaninterest.com/learn/articles/time-management-for-financial-advisors/)); offices spend **~13.5 hours per client per year** on the meeting lifecycle (prep, meeting, follow-up) ([GReminders](https://www.greminders.com/articles/financial-advisors-wealth-management/)).
- **Duplicate data entry is structural.** The same client info is keyed separately into CRM, planning tools, and portfolio systems ([Digital Alpha on Kitces 2025](https://www.digital-alpha.com/integration-before-ai-what-the-2025-kitces-research-really-says/)); average advisor satisfaction with integrations is **6.2/10** — "not a feature gap, a structural gap" (same source).
- **Tech dissatisfaction drives attrition.** Firms with high tech satisfaction: 1% of advisors at high risk of leaving within 5 years vs. **25% at low-satisfaction firms** — a 25× retention gap ([Digital Alpha/Kitces 2025](https://www.digital-alpha.com/integration-before-ai-what-the-2025-kitces-research-really-says/)).
- **Tech spend correlates with growth.** 2025 Schwab RIA Benchmarking: high-tech-spend firms grew AUM **16.6% vs 12.1%** for manual-workflow firms ([via Investipal](https://www.investipal.co/blog/7-technology-investments-financial-advisors-should-make-before-2026)).
- Key 2025 Kitces finding for positioning: **the biggest driver of tech satisfaction is integration / killing manual re-entry — not AI features per se** ([Digital Alpha](https://www.digital-alpha.com/integration-before-ai-what-the-2025-kitces-research-really-says/)). An AI-native CRM's pitch should be "the data enters itself," not "chat with your CRM."

## 4. AI trend in advisor tech, 2024–2026

- **AI notetakers went from novelty to majority in ~2 years.** T3/Inside Information 2026 (n=2,906): **14 AI notetaking solutions tracked (up from 1 in 2025), aggregate 42.9% share** and estimated >60% at survey publication; larger firms (> $8M revenue) at 43% adoption vs 32% for the smallest ([T3 2026 findings](https://t3technologyhub.com/live-from-t3-key-wealthtech-and-ai-findings-from-the-2026-t3-inside-information-software-survey/)). Broader gen-AI tool usage: **52% of advisors in 2026, up from 41% in 2025**, led by ChatGPT (40.9%) (same source).
- **Category leaders and funding:**
  - **Jump** (SLC, founded 2023): $20M Series A (Feb 2025, Battery) → **$80M Series B (Feb 2026, Insight Partners)**, total $105M, **27,000 advisors** on platform; explicitly expanding from notetaker to an "AI-native operating system / intelligence and action layer" for advisory firms ([Jump press](https://jump.ai/press/series-b-announcement); [WealthManagement.com](https://www.wealthmanagement.com/artificial-intelligence/jump_secures_series_b)).
  - **Zocks** (SF, founded 2022): $13.8M Series A → **$45M Series B (Jan 2026, Lightspeed + QED)**, $65M total; 1,000+ firms onboarded since Feb 2024 ([Crunchbase News](https://news.crunchbase.com/fintech/zocks-raises-seriesb-ai-assistant-financial-advisers/); [Financial Planning](https://www.financial-planning.com/news/ai-tools-jump-and-zocks-gain-funding-and-advisor-fans)).
  - **Zeplyn** ($3M seed, Leo Capital) has pivoted to "agentic AI" as **the notetaker market hits saturation** ([PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/zeplyn-raises-3m-seed-funding-for-ai-assistant-to-streamline-wealth-management-workflows-and-improve-client-experience-302303666.html); [InvestmentNews](https://www.investmentnews.com/advisor-tech/zeplyn-pivots-towards-agentic-ai-as-the-ai-notetaker-market-reaches-its-saturation-point/265655)).
  - Vertical tools beat horizontal ones on satisfaction: Jump/Zocks top Kitces satisfaction ratings while **Zoom AI Companion is #1 in adoption but dead last in satisfaction** ([Kitces AI notetaker research](https://www.kitces.com/blog/ai-notetakers-client-meeting-for-financial-advisors-adoption-satisfaction-trends-research-productivity/)). Solo advisors adopt most (no one to delegate to); heavy-planning advisors adopt at ~4× the rate of light-planning ones (same source).
- **AI-native CRM / "wealth OS" entrants:** Powder ($5M seed, YC — AI document analysis, claims 90% time saved reading docs) ([WealthManagement.com](https://www.wealthmanagement.com/financial-technology/wealthtech-startup-powder-raises-5m-in-seed-funding)); **Nevis** ($5M Sequoia seed 2024 → **~$35M Series A from Sequoia/ICONIQ/Ribbit in 2025**) ([Sequoia](https://sequoiacap.com/article/nevis-bringing-ai-to-wealth-management/)); Avantos ($25M Series A); Wavvest; Advisor360°'s "AI-native Wealth OS"; Claro's AI-native platform (Feb 2026) ([WealthManagement.com roundup](https://www.wealthmanagement.com/artificial-intelligence/advisor360-announces-ai-native-wealth-operating-system); [Morningstar/BusinessWire](https://www.morningstar.com/news/business-wire/20260212807388/claro-advisors-launches-ai-native-wealth-management-platform-for-independent-financial-advisors); [AdvisorCRM](https://advisorcrm.com/)).
- **Incumbents retrofitting:** Wealthbox shipped an AI meeting-note tool (4/2025) and "AI agents" (4/2026) ([Kitces AdvisorTech Apr 2025](https://www.kitces.com/blog/the-latest-in-financial-advisortech-april-2025-news-wealthbox-ai-meeting-note-tool-hubly-advisor360/), [Apr 2026](https://www.kitces.com/blog/the-latest-in-financial-advisortech-april-2026-wealthbox-ai-agents-tools-jump-rightcapital-wealthstream/)); Redtail/Orion expanding AI integrations and shipping AI assistants in 2026 ([Yahoo/WealthStack](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/wealthstack-roundup-expanded-ai-integrations-154518377.html); [WealthManagement.com](https://www.wealthmanagement.com/artificial-intelligence/orion-to-release-new-ai-assistants-for-advisors-in-2026)); AdvisorEngine integrated Jump/Zocks/Zeplyn directly ([AdvisorEngine](https://www.advisorengine.com/newsroom/advisorengine-now-works-with-popular-ai-notetakers)).
- **Sentiment vs. reality gap:** Accenture (n=500 NA advisors): 96% believe gen-AI is transformative, 78% of firms experimenting, but only **41% scaling** ([Accenture](https://www.accenture.com/us-en/insights/capital-markets/gen-ai-power-growth-wealth-managers)). Orion 2025 (n=571): ~75% of firms use AI somehow, but only **6% have agentic workflows and 5% cross-system AI integration** ([InvestmentNews](https://www.investmentnews.com/goria/technology/future-ready-advisors-bet-on-ai-integrated-tech-and-alts-orion-survey-finds/265434)). The whitespace is exactly "AI woven through the system of record," which is what an AI-native CRM is.

**Strategic implication:** the notetaker layer is saturated and its leaders (Jump, Zocks) are marching *down* into the CRM/OS layer with $45–80M war chests, while CRM incumbents march *up* with bolt-on AI. The contested ground is the AI-native system of record; timing is now, not in two years.

## 5. Trust / privacy angle

- **Fiduciary duty is the hard constraint.** "An investment adviser, as a fiduciary, has a duty to safeguard clients' confidential information… confirm that agreements with an AI vendor protect uploaded information from model training or unrelated processing" ([Morrison Foerster, Oct 2025](https://www.mofo.com/resources/insights/251015-ai-compliance-tips-for-advisers)). The FPA Journal flags that data exposed to gen-AI tools "can be used to train the model and potentially be discoverable by other users" ([FPA Journal, May 2025](https://www.financialplanningassociation.org/learning/publications/journal/MAY25-compliance-risks-using-generative-ai-financial-planning-practice-OPEN)).
- **Regulators have moved from silence to explicit expectations.** SEC added AI to its **2025 exam priorities**; FINRA's 2025 Oversight Report focused on AI ([Debevoise](https://www.debevoise.com/insights/publications/2025/02/finras-2025-regulatory-oversight-report-focus-on)); FINRA's **2026 Oversight Report added a dedicated GenAI section** covering governance, vendor/third-party LLM risk ("assess whether the cybersecurity program contemplates risks associated with third-party vendors' use of GenAI"), prompt/output logging and model-version recordkeeping, and **autonomous agents** — "agents operating on sensitive data may unintentionally store, explore, disclose or misuse sensitive or proprietary information," with human-in-the-loop controls recommended ([FINRA 2026 GenAI report](https://www.finra.org/rules-guidance/guidance/reports/2026-finra-annual-regulatory-oversight-report/gen-ai); [Smarsh](https://www.smarsh.com/blog/thought-leadership/ai-governance-expectations-are-rising-even-without-rules)).
- **Advisor and client sentiment back it up.** ~74% report data-privacy/security concerns around AI ([Halbert Hargrove](https://www.halberthargrove.com/news-guidance/ai-adoption-barriers-advisors/)); Janus Henderson: **79% of clients would be upset to learn an advisor used AI without telling them** ([American Banker](https://www.americanbanker.com/news/clients-put-advisors-on-notice-about-sneaky-ai-use)); Accenture: 77% of advisors cite data quality/transparency as the top responsible-AI barrier ([Accenture](https://www.accenture.com/us-en/insights/capital-markets/gen-ai-power-growth-wealth-managers)).
- **Self-hosted / open-weights precedent exists in finance.** BNP Paribas partnered with Mistral specifically to run models **on-premises for regulatory compliance**; the standard enterprise argument is that self-hosting gives "zero data egress — a crucial requirement in regulated environments," with Llama/Mistral/Qwen as the deployable families ([TrueFoundry](https://www.truefoundry.com/blog/on-prem-llms); [TechStoriess](https://www.techstoriess.com/open-source-on-premise-llms-deployment-security-cost-and-performance-compared/)).
- **Honest caveat:** among *independent RIAs* (vs. banks), the observed behavior is vendor-DPA-based comfort, not self-hosting — most firms use third-party AI vendors rather than building internally ([Sidley](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2025/02/artificial-intelligence-us-financial-regulator-guidelines-for-responsible-use)). The winning wedge is likely "no client data ever trains a foundation model / private-VPC or self-hosted inference option / full prompt-output audit logs mapped to FINRA's 2026 expectations" as a *sales differentiator*, strongest in enterprise/broker-dealer deals where InfoSec review is the gate.

## 6. Buyer & GTM notes

- **Two distinct buyers.**
  - *Solo/small RIA (the volume market):* self-serve, card-swipe, per-user SaaS. Wealthbox's $39–45/user/mo and dominance under $250k revenue defines the price envelope ([Kitces](https://www.kitces.com/blog/financial-advisor-software-survey-ratings-market-share-fintech-wealthtech-landscape-chart/); [SmartAsset](https://smartasset.com/advisor-resources/wealthbox-crm)). ACV per firm: ~$500–$3k. Notably, solo advisors are the *heaviest AI-notetaker adopters* ([Kitces](https://www.kitces.com/blog/ai-notetakers-client-meeting-for-financial-advisors-adoption-satisfaction-trends-research-productivity/)) — they feel the "no one to delegate to" pain the product solves.
  - *Enterprise (10+ advisors, broker-dealers, $1B+ RIAs):* Salesforce territory (>50% of 10+ advisor firms), $225–450/user/mo, consultant-mediated, 6–12 month cycles ([Kitces](https://www.kitces.com/blog/financial-advisor-software-survey-ratings-market-share-fintech-wealthtech-landscape-chart/); [Vantage Point](https://vantagepoint.io/blog/sf/the-complete-crm-showdown-salesforce-fsc-vs.-wealthbox-vs.-redtail-vs.-orion-vs.-practifi)). ACVs: tens of thousands to millions. $1B+ firms control 74% of RIA-channel assets ([Cerulli](https://www.cerulli.com/reports/us-ria-marketplace-2025)) — this is where Jump got to 27k advisors fast, via enterprise/BD rollouts ([Jump](https://jump.ai/press/series-b-announcement)).
- **Channels that actually move this market:** the **T3 conference + T3/Inside Information survey** and the **Kitces AdvisorTech map/blog** are the discovery layer for independents ([t3technologyhub.com](https://t3technologyhub.com/live-from-t3-key-wealthtech-and-ai-findings-from-the-2026-t3-inside-information-software-survey/); [Kitces AdvisorTech directory](https://fintech.kitces.com/category/operations-essentials/crm/)); platform/custodian integrations (Orion, AdvisorEngine, Schwab, planning tools) function as distribution — AdvisorEngine embedding Jump/Zocks/Zeplyn is the template ([AdvisorEngine](https://www.advisorengine.com/newsroom/advisorengine-now-works-with-popular-ai-notetakers)); trade press (InvestmentNews, WealthManagement.com, Financial Planning) covers every launch in this niche.
- **Switching dynamics:** CRM is sticky (system of record, migration pain), but churn is real and UI-driven — documented Redtail→Wealthbox migrations over interface frustration ([Softbliq](https://www.softbliq.com/redtail-crm-review/)), and Redtail's post-Orion pricing changes created a shopping moment ([RIA Operators](https://www.skool.com/ria-os/upset-about-new-redtail-pricing-considering-moving-to-wealthbox)). Migration tooling (import from Redtail/Wealthbox in a day) is a GTM feature, not a nicety.
- **Pricing posture suggestion from the data:** land at Wealthbox-plus ($60–100/user/mo) with the AI layer included — undercutting the CRM+Jump stack (~$40 + ~$100/user/mo) while 3–5× the incumbent CRM ARPU; enterprise tier priced against Practifi/FSC ($100–250/user/mo) with the compliance/audit/self-hosted-model story as the differentiator per FINRA 2026 expectations ([FINRA](https://www.finra.org/rules-guidance/guidance/reports/2026-finra-annual-regulatory-oversight-report/gen-ai)).

## Bottom line

A ~$0.5–1B/yr US wedge (multi-billion global category growing ~11–14%/yr) where the top incumbents are non-AI-native, the #1 documented pain (advisors at 41% client-facing time; 36% of time on meeting prep/follow-up; 6.2/10 integration satisfaction) is precisely what AI-native architecture fixes, the AI-notetaker wave has already trained >40–60% of advisors to talk to AI about client meetings, and the best-funded entrants (Jump $105M, Zocks $65M, Nevis ~$40M) are all converging on the same "AI-native system of record" thesis from adjacent positions. The privacy/compliance angle (no model training on client data, audit logs, private/self-hosted inference) is a credible enterprise differentiator now that FINRA 2026 has put GenAI governance and agent controls in writing.
